About

I am a first-year PhD student in computer science at the University of Rochester, advised by professors Dan Gildea and Aaron White. My research interests lie primarily in natural language understanding, and my current work is focused on reasoning about events in text as part of the Decompositional Semantics project.

Previously, I graduated cum laude from the wonderful Bowdoin College in 2017 with a B.A. in Computer Science and subsequently worked for two years as a software engineer at Okta in San Francisco. I am also a committed minimalist and effective altruist.

Projects

While at Bowdoin, I completed a senior honors thesis investigating the year-over-year impact of various environmental and economic variables on global crop yields using genetics-based machine learning (GBML) techniques. You can find this work on GitHub. My thesis advisor was Clare Bates Congdon. My major advisor was Stephen Majercik.
