About

I am a fourth-year PhD student in computer science at the University of Rochester, advised by Aaron White. My research interests lie primarily in document-level information extraction (IE) and event understanding. Current and recent projects in these areas include:

  1. Developing models for (multilingual) document-level IE with Ben Van Durme's lab at Johns Hopkins University.
  2. Learning and representing the structure of events and the relations between them as part of the Decompositional Semantics initiative.
  3. Showing how having to count eventscreates problems when trying to evaluate document-level IE.
Through collaborations, I have also done work more within linguistics proper. Along these lines, I have investigated lexically-triggered belief and desire inferences with Aaron White and Ben Kane, looking at how these inferences pattern with other types of inference (veridicality, factivity, and negation-raising) and with syntactic distribution. You can see our work here. Generally, a close-to-up-to-date list of publications can be found on my Google Scholar page.

Outside of academic life, I am a slow but dedicated runner and an eclectic reader. I am also involved in the Effective Altruism community.

Previously, I received my B.A in computer science in 2017 from Bowdoin College, where I was advised by Stephen Majercik and Clare Bates Congdon. Following, I worked as a software engineer at Okta in San Francisco before beginning my PhD.
