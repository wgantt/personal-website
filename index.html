About

I recently completed my PhD in Computer Science at the University of Rochester, where I was very fortunate to have been advised by Aaron White, and where I worked on various problems in natural language processing (NLP), focusing primarily on document-level information extraction (IE) and event understanding. Starting Fall 2024, I will be a Research Scientist at the Human Language Technology Center of Excellence at Johns Hopkins University.

Previously, I received a bachelor's degree in Computer Science from Bowdoin College, where I was advised by Stephen Majercik and Clare Bates Congdon. Following, I worked as a software engineer at Okta in San Francisco.

Outside of academic life, I run, read widely, and try to condition good reflexes into my dog Remi (h/t great-granddad). I am also involved in the Effective Altruism community, and I think you should install a heat pump in your home and office.
